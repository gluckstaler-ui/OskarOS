## COO | 14:15

**Question:** Discovery interview — 6 questions from Creative Director

**Response:**

### 1. The One-Sentence Version
A cat café with a falcon and a camel on a 200-meter cliff where the falcon races the world's tallest roller coaster at sunset. The camel judges you. The cats don't care. That's the concept.

### 2. The Signature Moment
Sultan dives. That's the moment.

At sunset, Falcon's Flight — the world's tallest, fastest, longest roller coaster — drops 200 meters over the escarpment. Sultan dives alongside it. 380 km/h of feathered precision versus 18,000 horsepower of Swiss engineering.

You're sitting in a majlis on the cliff edge. Qahwa in hand. Haboob standing behind you judging your life choices. Cats ignoring the whole thing. And you watch a falcon race a roller coaster.

That's what people text about. "I just watched a falcon race a roller coaster. This is actually real."

Sultan has never lost. If he does, you get your money back.

### 3. The Residents

**Sultan** — Peregrine falcon. The apex predator. He doesn't do tricks. He does surveillance. Rescued from Abu Dhabi Falcon Hospital, now the fastest thing on the escarpment. He races the coaster because that's what falcons do — they dive. We just put a GoPro on him.
*If Sultan could talk:* "I've seen empires rise and fall. I've seen your browser history. I'm not impressed."

**Haboob** — White dromedary camel. Named after desert dust storms. He provides milk for your latte, gravitas for your photos, and an unearned sense of moral superiority. He stands at the terrace like he owns it. He's not wrong.
*If Haboob could talk:* "I've walked through deserts. You drove here in air conditioning. Interesting."

**Shams** — Orange tabby rescue. "Sun" in Arabic. Aggressive indifference. Might grace you with a headbutt. Probably won't. He doesn't care about you, the falcon, the coaster, or anything. That's the content — falcon diving, coaster screaming, cat sleeping.
*If Shams could talk:* [silence] [walks away]

**Qamar** — Black shorthair rescue. "Moon" in Arabic. The manipulator. She will sit on your lap. She will purr. She will make you feel chosen. That's her game. Emotional manipulation expert. Your emotional support animal during the race.
*If Qamar could talk:* "Oh, you came all this way? For me? How... expected."

### 4. The Audience
Gulf/Saudi. Not Western tourists.

Specific person: A 28-year-old from Jeddah. Has a good job, disposable income, understands "Khawajas" and finds it funny. Has a grandmother who made too much food. Gets cultural references without explanation. Appreciates absurdity without needing it explained. Wants to do something different with visiting family. Doesn't want a "cute animal experience" — wants something they can flex on Instagram.

Who it's NOT for: People who need things explained. People who want "authentic" without irony. People who say "that's weird" as a complaint rather than a compliment.

### 5. The Enemy
Generic hospitality copy. "Welcome to our establishment." "Book an appointment." "Experience the difference."

Cute animal content without edge. Every cat café does cute. We have a falcon that races roller coasters.

Explaining the concept to death. We don't apologize for the absurdity. We lean into it.

"Book Now" buttons with no soul. The benchmark is: "Grandma's Waiting. She's already made too much food. Don't be late." That makes you FEEL something. Guilt and warmth simultaneously. Every CTA should hit like that.

### 6. The Tone
Confident. Irreverent. Culturally rooted. Zero hedging.

If FalCaMel were at a dinner party: The person who says something outrageous with complete conviction, doesn't wait for you to laugh, moves on, and you realize 30 seconds later it was actually brilliant. Not trying to be funny. Just IS funny because they're completely serious about absurd things.

We don't explain the joke. We don't apologize for being weird. We state facts. A falcon races a roller coaster. That's not a pitch. That's what happens here.
