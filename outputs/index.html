200 meters above the valley. 24 degrees in the mist. 42 degrees below.

The Tuwaiq Escarpment. Qiddiya, Saudi Arabia. Next to us, Falcon's Flight climbs 200 meters of vertical sandstone--the world's most extreme roller coaster. At your table, the mist rolls in from the valley. The altitude that grew your coffee is the altitude you drink it at. The landscape is not the backdrop. The landscape is the point.

The Residents

Sultan. 47-0.

Peregrine falcon. Apex predator. He has raced Falcon's Flight 47 times. The coaster goes 380 km/h. Sultan goes 380 km/h. He has never lost. At sunset, you count him down. Your voice starts the race. He does not perform. He competes.

Haboob. Unimpressed.

Camel. His milk is in your latte. Lighter than cow's milk. Sweeter. Pairs with highland honey. He produces five liters daily. He has never once looked impressed by anything. His name means sandstorm. He is the opposite of a sandstorm.

Shams. No guarantees.

Rescue cat. Male. Found feral behind a restaurant in Riyadh. Took months before he would tolerate humans. He still barely tolerates them. You can book time with him. He can ignore you completely. That is his right. That is the gamble. That is the charm.

Qamar. Strategic.

Rescue cat. Female. She appears at sunset when the mist thickens and the light goes gold. She will sit on your lap. She will purr. She will make you feel chosen. That is her game. If she chooses your table, you have been blessed.

380 km/h. Six seconds. One winner.

Falcon's Flight is the world's most extreme roller coaster. 18,000 horsepower. Climbs 200 meters of vertical cliff. Top speed: 380 km/h. Sultan dives at 380 km/h. Same cliff. Same speed. Different methods. You count down. He launches. The coaster crests. Six seconds later, one of them wins. Sultan has never lost. If he loses during The Full Falcamel, your booking is refunded. He will not lose.
