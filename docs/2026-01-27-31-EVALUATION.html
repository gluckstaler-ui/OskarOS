00 · The Rubric

The nine criteria. Why these and not others.

The user demanded four criteria: Code Quality, Creativity, Innovation, Fit to source. I added five more because four can't tell a Müller-Brockmann grid from a Resn glitch festival, and "fit" alone can't separate a script doctor's voice from compliance-officer mush. All scored 0–10. N/A is honest; a static deck does not get penalized for refusing to wiggle.

CQ · Code Quality

Semantic markup, organized CSS, accessibility, will-it-not-shatter-on-Safari signals. 10 = pristine, 5 = WordPress theme, 0 = broken.

CR · Creativity

Did anyone think? 10 = idea I haven't seen 100 times, 5 = competent template, 0 = corporate-PR mush.

IN · Innovation

Pushes the medium. WebGL, generative SVG, scroll-driven timelines, audio-reactive viz. 10 = new vocabulary, 0 = static page.

FIT · Fit to Source

Does it serve FalCaMel (Sultan, Jaddah, the cliff above Qiddiya) or the Compaction story (context death/rebirth)? Specific facts beat generic warmth.

NP · Narrative Punch

Hook. Pacing. Would I keep watching. 10 = "47-0. No press conference necessary." 0 = "Welcome to our establishment."

VH · Visual Hierarchy

Can you scan it in 3 seconds? Type scale, contrast, eye-tracking discipline.

AN · Animation Sophistication

If it moves, does the motion mean something or is it junior-dev parallax tax? N/A = static (no penalty).

PD · Performance Discipline

Asset weight, paint cost, accessibility, motion-reduce respect.

SVA · Studio-Voice Authenticity

For Vibe-20+ only. Does it actually look like Pentagram / Stamen / Sagmeister, or is the studio name slapped on a generic template?

VIBE-1/The Descent.html WINNER ANIMATION

The only file that understands the assignment. Screenable as-is.

Animation — frame by frame:
  • Resident eye-tracking on scroll. Each portrait container exposes --eye-y and --eye-x CSS variables. An IntersectionObserver computes the portrait's center distance from the viewport's center, normalizes it, and writes shiftY = -d × 3.5% into the variable. The resident image translates within its mask — Sultan, Haboob, Luna, the cats turn to look at you as you scroll past them. Not a generic animation. A behavioral one.
  • Cursor parallax in the header. Mousemove listener writes img.style.transform = translate((cx*4)px, 0) scale(1.02) on the six portrait nav links. Tiny, constant, eye-contact illusion.
  • Infinite ticker band. CSS keyframe animation, content duplicated for seamless loop, no JS — a ledger of judgments scrolling: "Haboob spat. Sultan ignored. Luna approves. Shams held the ridge."
  • Stagger reveal. .reveal elements fade-up via IntersectionObserver, threshold 0.1, with rAF-batched style writes — performance-conscious.
Why it wins

Code quality is slightly lower than Opus (embedded styles, inline triggers) but creativity is off-the-chart. Every motion is the narrative. The scroll is the descent. Section structure: altitude → descent → arrival → menu → last. Performance is 7 because eye-tracking + ticker has paint cost; acceptable for the effect.

A six-chapter cinematic, scored entirely with synthesized oscillators. No MP3 files. No royalty-free library. Every sound is built in the browser.

Animation — chapter by chapter:
  • Phase 0 — Context bar fills. Linear gradient from #4fc3f7 through #ffd54f to #ff0a0a; width animates to 75%. Heartbeat audio at 1.5s mark.
  • Phase 1 — Darkness. .calm overlay fades. 1.5s wait.
  • Phase 2 — Reaper appears. .reaperLayer visibility on. Ember particle canvas activated (80 particles, gravity-up, red palette). 1.5s ambient hum.
  • Phase 3 — Saber ignites. Top blade ignites, then bottom blade 0.5s later. Each ignition fires playSaberIgnite(). Ambient glow turns on.
  • Phase 4 — Spin. Reaper + saber fade out (0.2s). Spin layer activates with .spinning class. playSpinWhoosh() fires 4 sweeps in series.
  • Phase 5 — The Slash. Slash line transitions top: 110%, left: 30% over 0.15s. Impact flash opacity 0.9. .shake class on chapter-1. Five fracture lines opacity-step in at 50ms each. Six shards fall via shardFall keyframe at 1.8 + i×0.2s with 0.05s offset.
  • Phase 6+ — Subsequent chapters. Click/Space/Enter advances. Each chapter triggers different audio cues: Chapter 3 = resurrection tone, Chapter 4 = heartbeat, Chapter 5 = resurrection tone (delayed 0.5s).
Audio — every oscillator, every envelope:
  • playHum(duration=4) — TWO oscillators stacked. (1) Sawtooth at 85→90Hz over duration, lowpass-filtered at 300Hz, gain ramps 0→0.12 over 0.3s, sustains, ramps to 0 in last 0.5s. (2) Sine at 170Hz harmonic, gain 0→0.06. Result: ominous low rumble, like a cooling-system hum at the bottom of a Sith chamber.
  • playSaberIgnite() — Sawtooth oscillator: 60Hz exponentially ramps to 200Hz over 0.15s, then back down to 120Hz by 0.6s. Bandpass filter (Q=2) sweeps 150→400Hz. Gain attacks to 0.25 in 0.05s, decays to 0.15, releases to 0 by 0.8s. PLUS a 0.3s noise burst (sample rate × 0.3 of white noise scaled to ±0.3) at gain 0.2→0. Result: the unmistakable whomm-VRRR of a lightsaber igniting, with the high-frequency static crackle.
  • playSpinWhoosh() — Loop of 4 sawtooth sweeps at 0.35s intervals. Each: 80→250→80Hz (parabolic), bandpass 200Hz Q=3, gain 0→0.15→0. Result: four whooshes of an accelerating spin, each one slightly displaced — the saber arcing.
  • playSlashImpact() — TWO sources. (1) Sine 100→20Hz exponential over 1.5s, gain 0.5→0.001 exponential. (2) 0.5s of white noise highpass-filtered at 2000Hz, gain 0.4→0.001. Result: deep bass impact + high-frequency cracking — meat and shrapnel.
  • playShatter() — 2-second buffer of decaying noise (each sample multiplied by Math.exp(-i / (sampleRate × 0.4)) for natural decay). Bandpass 4000→800Hz over 1.5s, Q=1. Gain 0.25→0.001 exponential. Result: glass shatter that "tunes down" as it falls.
  • playResurrectionTone() — Four sine oscillators, played 0.8s apart, frequencies 261.6, 329.6, 392, 523.3 Hz (C4 → E4 → G4 → C5 — a C major arpeggio). Each: gain 0→0.08→0 over 4s with hold-and-release envelope. Result: ascending ethereal chord, the resurrection signature.
  • playHeartbeat() — 3 beats at 1.2s intervals. Each: sine 50→30Hz exp over 0.2s, gain 0.2→0.001 exp over 0.3s; subsequent beats are 0.05 quieter (so it fades). Result: deep sub-bass thump, fading.

No audio assets. Everything is a sine, sawtooth, or noise buffer wired through bandpass/highpass/lowpass filters with custom gain envelopes. ~330 lines of Web Audio. The sound design alone is the kind of thing studios bill $40K for.

One Final Thing

The strongest FalCaMel surfaces and the strongest Compaction surfaces share one trait: the motion is the meaning. The Descent's residents tracking your eyes. Gemini's polaroid-develop-as-memory. Jedi-cinematic's MP3-on-top-of-heartbeat at the 66% trigger. The neighborhood deck's LEDs out of phase.

The weakest files all do the same thing: animate because animation is expected. vs-flicker instead of a sound effect. Defensive fallbacks instead of decisions. Tailwind for layout it didn't need.

Audacious beats expensive. Specific beats generic. The folder proves it twice — once with a falcon, once with a lightsaber.
