1. The thesis — what "state-of-the-art" means now

A modern sales-funnel CRM is no longer a database with a kanban board on top. It is a system of work: it ingests signals from outside the company (intent data, website behavior, calls, emails, LinkedIn), routes them through programmable automation, surfaces the right next action to a rep at the right moment, and proves what worked.

The five non-negotiable shifts

  • Pipeline as a verb, not a noun — the system tells the rep what to do, doesn't just store deals.
  • AI is plumbing, not a button — call summaries, drafted emails, deal scoring baked into every screen.
  • Data is enriched on arrival — every contact and company auto-hydrated from Clearbit/Apollo/built-in.
  • The funnel is multiplayer — AE, SDR, marketing, CS, ops all live in the same record.
  • Customization is no-code — admins build views, automations, custom objects without engineering.

What this means for design

  • Every record screen has an activity timeline as the spine, not a sidebar.
  • The kanban pipeline is one view among many, not the home base.
  • A persistent AI command bar lives across every surface.
  • The "my day" / task queue replaces the dashboard as the rep's home.
  • Reporting moves from a separate app to inline insights on every list and record.

3. Part 1 — Feature inventory

Eleven functional areas. Inside each, the specific features a designer needs to know exist before sketching screens.

3.1 Data model & records

1.1
Standard objects
Leads, Contacts, Accounts (Companies), Deals, Activities, Tasks, Notes, Files. Attio and Folk skip "leads" entirely.
1.2
Custom objects
User-defined record types (Properties, Loans, Projects). Salesforce, HubSpot Enterprise, Attio.
1.3
Custom fields
Text, number, picklist, multi-select, formula, lookup, currency, date, checkbox, URL, rich text, with validation.
1.4
Relationships & lookups
Many-to-many junctions (Contacts↔Deals). Hierarchical account trees.
1.5
Lifecycle stages
Subscriber → Lead → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Customer → Evangelist (HubSpot's canonical version).
1.6
Field history & audit
Every field change tracked, who/when/old/new. Required for compliance.
1.7
Merge & dedupe
Fuzzy-match suggestions, field-level merge UI.
1.8
Enrichment
Auto-populate company size, industry, tech stack, funding from Clearbit/Apollo on creation.
1.9
Data hygiene scoring
% complete records, stale-field flags, missing-required-field counts.

3.2 Pipeline & deal management

2.1
Multiple pipelines
Per product line, region, or sales motion (Inbound, Outbound, Expansion). Independent stages each.
2.2
Configurable stages
Names, order, probabilities, required fields per stage, entry/exit criteria.
2.3
Kanban board view
Drag-and-drop deal cards across stages. Pipedrive's signature UI.
2.4
Deal cards
Compact: amount, close date, owner, days-in-stage, health, next activity.
2.5
Forecast categories
Commit / Best Case / Pipeline / Omitted overlay.
2.6
Deal scoring / health
AI-driven traffic-light based on engagement, idle time, missing data. Gong, Einstein.
2.7
Rotting / idle flags
Deal idle > N days → automatic flag and reminder.
2.8
Weighted pipeline
Stage probability × amount = weighted forecast.
2.9
Won/lost reasons
Structured picklists on close, with required notes for loss analysis.
2.10
Products / line items
Multi-product deals with quantity, unit price, discount, total. Feeds CPQ.

3.3 Prospecting & lead generation

3.1
Built-in B2B database
270M+ contact records filterable by title, industry, headcount, tech stack, funding. Apollo's flagship.
3.2
List building
Save-as-list, refresh-on-schedule, share, bulk add-to-sequence.
3.3
Intent & signal feeds
Job-change, funding, technographic, web-intent, PLG-product triggers.
3.4
LinkedIn / Chrome ext
One-click capture from LinkedIn or Sales Navigator. Add-to-sequence inline.
3.5
Web forms & landing pages
Native form builder → auto-lead → routing rules. HubSpot's home turf.
3.6
Lead scoring
Rules-based or predictive ML. Einstein, HubSpot Predictive.
3.7
Lead routing
Round-robin, territory, account-based, weighted-by-quota.
3.8
Verified email / phone
Live email verification, mobile number resolution. Apollo, ZoomInfo, Clay.

3.4 Sales engagement (the cockpit)

4.1
Sequences / cadences
Multi-step, multi-channel: Email → Wait → LinkedIn → Wait → Call.
4.2
Sequence builder UI
Visual canvas with branching (replied → exit, opened → call). Outreach's classic.
4.3
Day View / task queue
Rep's home screen: today's calls, emails, LinkedIns in priority order. The most-used surface.
4.4
Click-to-call / power dialer
Built-in phone, local presence, parallel dialer. Close, Outreach Voice.
4.5
Call recording & transcription
Auto-record, transcribe, speaker-separate, searchable.
4.6
Email tracking
Open / click / reply. Routed via rep's mailbox for deliverability.
4.7
Templates & snippets
Reusable templates with merge fields and A/B variants.
4.8
SMS / WhatsApp
Native two-way, opt-in management, threading.
4.9
Meeting scheduler
Calendly-style booking, round-robin, qualifying questions.
4.10
Inbox / shared mailbox
Conversations view: every reply across team, threaded. Front-style.

3.5 Conversation intelligence

5.1
Call library
Every call searchable, taggable, shareable. Filter by deal, rep, outcome.
5.2
AI call summary
Auto-generated TL;DR, action items, mentioned competitors, pricing, objections.
5.3
Talk-time / talk-ratio
% rep vs customer per call/rep/deal.
5.4
Topic / tracker detection
Auto-detect mentions of "pricing", "competitor X", "next steps", "legal".
5.5
Deal warnings
"Champion went dark", "competitor mentioned 3x" — Gong's signature.
5.6
Coaching scorecards
Manager rubric per call. Inline comments on transcript.
5.7
Battlecards
Competitor mention → surface battlecard live during call.

3.6 AI & intelligence layer

6.1
AI command bar
Persistent CMD+K — "What's at risk this Q?" or "Draft a follow-up to David". Attio, Breeze, Einstein.
6.2
Email drafting
Generate a contextual email (knows the deal, last touch, persona). Edit-then-send.
6.3
Account research auto-brief
One-paragraph "what you need to know about Acme": news, hiring, posts, tech stack.
6.4
Predictive scoring
ML lead / deal / churn risk score. Explainable.
6.5
Next-best-action
Per contact/deal — "Send case study", "Schedule check-in", "Loop in CS".
6.6
Forecast assist
AI-adjusted forecast vs rep's commit. Explainable delta.
6.7
Smart note-taking
Meeting bot transcribes, writes the CRM note, fills custom fields.

3.7 Workflow & automation

7.1
Visual workflow builder
Trigger → branches → actions canvas. Salesforce Flow, HubSpot Workflows, Attio Automations.
7.2
Triggers
Field change, stage change, time, form submit, webhook, signal, anniversary.
7.3
Actions
Create record, update field, send email, post Slack, create task, enroll sequence, call webhook.
7.4
Approvals
Discount > 20% → manager. Approval queue UI.
7.5
Playbooks
Pre-built workflow bundles (post-demo follow-up, lost-deal nurture).
7.6
Notification rules
Rep pinged when account opens 3 emails or VP visits pricing.

3.8 Reporting & analytics

8.1
Funnel / conversion
Stage-by-stage % and time-in-stage. Where deals leak.
8.2
Forecast report
Commit vs Best Case vs Closed by period, rolled by team.
8.3
Pipeline movement
Created / advanced / slipped / closed / lost waterfall.
8.4
Activity reports
Calls/emails/meetings per rep, response rates, sequence performance.
8.5
Cohort analysis
Deals opened in March — where are they now? Win rate by source/segment.
8.6
Custom dashboards
Drag-and-drop widgets, shareable, scheduled email digest.
8.7
Report builder
Pivot-style: rows, columns, filters, measures. Salesforce Lightning is canonical.
8.8
Attribution
First / last / multi-touch revenue → marketing source.

3.9 Collaboration & communication

9.1
@mentions everywhere
In notes, comments, tasks. Attio, HubSpot, Salesforce Chatter.
9.2
Deal rooms / shared spaces
External buyer + seller space — mutual action plan, files, recap. Dock, DealHub.
9.3
Slack / Teams integration
Deal channel auto-created, updates piped, slash commands.
9.4
Internal handoff
SDR → AE, AE → CS — structured handoff form.
9.5
Real-time presence
"Sarah is viewing this deal right now." Notion / Attio style.

3.10 Admin, permissions, governance

10.1
Roles & hierarchies
Org chart with manager-sees-team rollup permissions.
10.2
Permission sets
Object-level, field-level, record-level (Salesforce gold standard).
10.3
Sharing rules
"West reps see East accounts read-only" — visual matrix.
10.4
SSO & SCIM
SAML, OIDC, auto-provisioning from Okta/Azure.
10.5
Audit log
Every admin action, login, export. SOC 2 / ISO requirements.
10.6
Sandbox
Test workflows without breaking production.
10.7
Data import / export
CSV wizard, column mapping, dedupe-on-import, scheduled exports.
10.8
GDPR / consent
Opt-in/out tracking, right-to-be-forgotten, regional data residency.

3.11 Integrations & extensibility

11.1
App marketplace
AppExchange, HubSpot Marketplace. 1,000+ pre-built integrations.
11.2
REST API + webhooks
Every object CRUDable, event subscriptions for change feeds.
11.3
Native email/calendar sync
Gmail, Outlook, M365. Two-way, real-time.
11.4
Data warehouse sync
Reverse ETL from Snowflake/BigQuery → CRM.
11.5
Mobile app
iOS/Android with offline mode, voice notes, biz-card scan.
11.6
No-code builder
Page layout editor, custom views, formula fields — all without code.

5. Part 3 — Seven screens, mocked in detail

SVG wireframes for the screens that disproportionately define product experience. Hover details, interaction notes, and best-in-class commentary follow each.

5.1 A1 — Day View (the rep's home)

The single most important screen in a modern sales tool. If reps don't open this every morning, the product has failed.

A1 · Day View — ① persistent AI command bar ② color-coded health rail per task ③ contextual right panel updates with focused task
Outreach — the original keyboard-driven queue

Outreach groups by task type with single-keystroke flow (E for email, C for call, S for skip). The rep never leaves keyboard.

Apollo.io — task queue with AI drafts inline

Apollo adds an AI-drafted email pane that prefills as the rep scrolls, so opening "Email" is one keystroke from sending.

Close — calls-first variant

Close puts a power dialer above the queue — clicking "Start dialing" runs through the call list with auto-advance and after-call disposition.

Must-have ingredients: grouped-by-channel pills · one-keystroke skip/complete · per-task color rail for health · expandable AI brief inline · contextual right panel · keyboard shortcut overlay accessible from header.

5.2 A2 — Pipeline Kanban (the classic)

Everyone has one. Modern differentiators: density configurability, deal-card information layering, inline edit, real-time presence.

A2 · Pipeline Kanban — colored side rails encode deal health · days-in-stage badge · column header carries count + sum · real-time presence in footer
Pipedrive — the gold standard for density

Pipedrive's deal cards show only what matters and use whitespace ruthlessly. Days-in-stage as a colored corner badge is theirs.

Attio — the visual upgrade

Attio inherits Notion-level polish: real-time presence avatars on cards, drag with springy physics, custom card layouts per pipeline.

5.3 B3 — Deal Record (the spine)

Every CRM has one. They almost all get it wrong. The modern version is three columns with timeline as the centerpiece, not a tab.

B3 · Deal Record — 3 columns: details + health + NBA · timeline as spine (not a tab) · people column shows buying-committee gaps explicitly
Gong Deal Page — health warnings above the fold

Gong's contribution to the canon: deal warnings are real estate above the fold, with explainability. "Why is this red?" — "Buyer went silent + competitor mentioned 3x last call."

Attio Deal Record — Notion-quality interactions

Three-column with center timeline is now best-practice. Attio adds inline edit on any field, @mention any teammate, drag-to-reorder.

HubSpot Deal — engagement heatmap on contacts

HubSpot shows a per-contact engagement heatmap, distinguishing buying committee members visually — the "unmet CFO" pattern.

5.4 A4 — Forecast Board

Where AEs and managers commit numbers. Modern UX shows AI-adjusted forecast next to human commit so the conversation is "why the delta?", not "what's the number?"

A4 · Forecast Board — four buckets (Closed · Commit · Best Case · Pipeline) · AI-forecast column with delta against rep's commit · "why?" link opens explainability panel
Clari — the category leader

Clari pioneered the AI-forecast-vs-commit pattern. Their secret was making the delta a click-through that shows which deals AI thinks won't close and why.

Salesforce Forecasts — the OG

Hierarchical roll-up across managers with adjustment locks. Less AI, more governance.

5.5 A6 — Dialer / Call Screen

The SDR/AE's call cockpit. Account context, live notes, transcription, talk-track suggestions, after-call disposition — all without leaving the screen.

A6 · Dialer — three-pane: account context · live transcript · AI coach with battlecards · topic detection writes back to deal automatically
Close — the inside-sales standard

Close's dialer is the model: power dialer, local presence, voicemail drop, after-call disposition modal forced on every call.

Gong / Chorus — the real-time AI coaching layer

Both ship live talk-track suggestions and competitor battlecards triggered by keyword detection on the live transcript.

5.6 A8 — Signals / Intent Feed

The new-gen entry point. Surfaces "this account is buying now" moments — funding events, job changes, web visits, PLG product usage. The screen that PLG-era CRMs (Common Room, Pocus, Default) are built around.

A8 · Signals Feed — typed pills filter signal categories · each card carries score + CRM context + one primary action · negative signals (CTO leaving) trigger red action
Common Room — the original signal aggregator

Built for PLG / community. Surfaces who's engaging with your community/docs/free tier and lets you trigger sales play.

Default / Pocus — PLG-native CRMs

Default treats signals as first-class records; Pocus puts a "PQL score" on every signal and lets ops build scoring rules visually.

5.7 C13 — Workflow / Automation Builder

The screen that makes the product programmable. Almost no rep ever opens it, but ops admins live here.

C13 · Workflow Builder — block library left · canvas center with diamond branches · inspector right showing live performance stats for the selected node
Salesforce Flow — the most powerful, the ugliest

Capable of running entire businesses; the UI is feared. Modern Salesforce orgs hire dedicated Flow architects.

HubSpot Workflows — the most approachable

Linear-ish layout, branching collapsed by default. Good defaults for sales-ops people who aren't engineers.

Attio Automations — the modern attempt

Visual-flow editor that feels like Linear or Notion. Less powerful than Salesforce Flow but 5× easier to use.

7. Anti-patterns to avoid

The mistakes

  • Dashboard-as-homepage. Reps don't want pie charts at 9am — they want a queue.
  • Pipeline as the only deal view. Kanban breaks at >200 deals. Always offer list mode.
  • AI as a separate page. The copilot is a sidebar or command bar, never a "/ai" route.
  • Tabs on the record screen. Modern records use scroll-stitched sections, not tabs that hide state.
  • "Stage" as the only deal health signal. Add days-in-stage, engagement, multi-threading, idle.
  • Form-style edit screens. Every field should be inline-editable on hover. Edit modals are a 2010 UX.
  • Required-field walls. Make field requirements stage-gated, not creation-gated.
  • "Search" that matches one object type. Universal search across contacts, companies, deals, notes is table stakes.

The bar to clear

  • Pipeline loads in < 800ms for 500 deals.
  • Every record has one-click jump from email plugin and Slack.
  • Custom fields take < 60s to add (admin) and appear immediately (rep).
  • AI summary on any call < 2 min after upload.
  • Mobile pipeline is one-handed-thumb usable.
  • Keyboard shortcut to every primary action in A1, A2, B3.
  • List views support 50,000-row datasets without virtualization breaking.
  • Every feature has a no-code path AND an API path.
