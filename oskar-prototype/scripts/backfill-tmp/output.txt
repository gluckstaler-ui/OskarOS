## Taste Profile

Steve's first instinct is final. Across 20+ image variant evaluations, v1 held every single time — including four rounds on the AT-AT art detail and four rounds on his own portrait. The only moment of hesitation: the Blue Milk Sour gradient ("gorgeous, close call"), and he still picked v1. He evaluates fast, decides clean, doesn't revisit.

The hierarchy: **cinematic over clean, intimate over spectacular, tight over wide.** Amber underlighting beat natural light for his portrait. Mid-laugh beat polished headshot — "This isn't a headshot — this is the guy who built a Millennium Falcon in Niederdorf." A couple in a smuggler booth beat spacecraft immersion for the evening vibe. A tighter crop of AT-AT legs against pines beat showing more of the walker. Every choice pulls toward the specific and felt, away from the composed and correct.

Craft must be visible at the material level. Canvas weave, impasto brushstrokes, riveted panels, working cockpit switches. The texture is the proof. He commissioned original oil paintings from Sommerhalder (AT-AT in Engadin, X-Wings over Matterhorn, Tauntaun in Appenzell) — not to decorate a themed café but to make art that lives at the intersection of Swiss landscape and Star Wars. The CSS painting frame with gallery attribution was flagged as setting the right tone. This is a gallery-owner instinct applied to a café.

Interactive and tactile details surface unprompted — cockpit bar with working light-up switches, handbuilt lightsaber prize from a craftsman in Winterthur. These are objects with provenance, not props with price tags.

APPROVED: Intimacy, imperfection, warmth earned through texture and story.
PATTERN: He never once chose the "cleaner" or "more polished" variant. Rough magnetism beats composed beauty every time.

Tab labels that carry voice ("The Roast / After Dark / The Kitchen") — this is the quality floor for copy. Navigation should sound like the place, not describe the place.

### CD-MEMORY.MD

**[2026-04-10] Image injection gap pattern:**
When WebDev builds vibes before images are generated, the images are never retroactively injected. The build system doesn't have a "hot-swap" step — once HTML is written, images must be explicitly mapped in the brief and a rebuild triggered. **Fix for all sessions:** Image Map sections with exact filenames must exist in the brief BEFORE triggering any vibe build. If images arrive after build, a rebuild with explicit image maps is required — don't assume they'll be picked up automatically.

**[2026-04-10] IMAGES.md format sensitivity:**
The image prompt parser requires `**bold:**` field names. Plain text field names cause `extractField()` to return null silently — no error, just empty results. Three fix attempts were needed before this was identified. All agents writing to IMAGES.md must use bold field formatting.

**[2026-04-10] Fabrication pattern:**
WebDev and CD both fabricated plausible-sounding details (building years, visitor nationalities, geography facts) when filling template slots. Three separate fabrication incidents in one session. **Rule for all agents:** If a fact isn't in the creative brief or discovery answers, it doesn't exist. Leave the slot empty or use a generic placeholder rather than inventing specifics.
